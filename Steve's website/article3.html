Simple exercises that gets you abs in no time

26-Mar-2021 By StayFit Admin

While having a six pack is great, there are plenty other reasons to train and get strong abs too. Strong core is the foundation for many other things, such as helping to maintain good posture to prevent lower back pain or the extra mobility and strenght it provides to excel in sports.

  1. Planking

    While keeping a straight line from the shoulders to the toes, support yourself on your forearms and toes. For beginner, start with 20 seconds of holding, eventually increasing the duration.

  2. Reverse Crunch

    While lying down, lift legs so your thighs are perpendicular to the ground and your knees bent at 90 degree angle. This is your start position. Contract your abs to bring your knees to your chest then release to return back to start. For begineer start with doing 10 cycles.

  3. Deadbug

    While lying down, put your hands up and straight pointing at the ceiling, lift both legs up so that your thighs are perpendicular to the ground and your ankle is bent at 90 degress angle. This is your start position. Then slowly extend and lower right leg until heel nearly touches floor and left arm until hand nearly touches the floor at the same time. Pause, then return to start and repeat for the other side. For beginner, start with doing 5 cycles.

  4. Leg raise

    As you get stronger abs you can try out harder exercises like leg raise to train your abs. To do a leg raise, lie down flat, back on the ground. Then lift both legs at the same time, while ensuring you keep a straigt leg, then back to lower position. At any time, the heels should not be touching the ground. For a start, you can do 10 cycles of this.

  5. Flutter kicks

    To do flutter kicks,lie down flat, back on the ground. then lift one leg at a time, the idea is to 'kick the air' while lying on the ground. Ensure that the legs are straight while doing this exercise and also that the heels should not touch the ground at any point.

  6. Bicyle Crunch

    Lie on your back, extend your legs and hold them just above the ground. Place your fingers lightly on your temples. Bring one knee up towards your chest and twist your torso so that the opposite elbow comes over towards it.   7. Seated Rotation

    Sit on the floor and lean upper body back until abs are engaged. Legs should be lifted and bent at 90 degrees, and arms should be bent, hands clasped, with elbows wide. This is your starting position. Rotate torso to right side so that right elbow is hovering just off mat. Keep lower body still while rotating upper body to left side until left elbow is just off mat. Return to start. Gaze follows hands as you move. That's one rep.
